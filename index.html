Indications for Women

  • Hirsutism (upper lip, chin, hair on the abdomen and chest)
  • Hypertrichosis (hair on the back, neck, other areas)
  • Hygienic areas (bikini area, armpits)
  • Aesthetic areas (arms, legs, eyebrow area)
  • Presence of discomfort affecting personal self-esteem

Indications for men

  • Need for constant shaving of facial areas (beard, mustache, sideburns, eyebrows)
  • You need to contour facial hair
  • You want to address problems with ingrown hair
  • Excessive hair on the back and chest
  • You spend a lot of time on daily shaving procedures

Contraindications for the Procedure

  • Inflammatory processes in the body
  • Pregnancy or breastfeeding
  • Presence of metal in the body
  • Decompensated diabetes mellitus
  • Damage to the epidermis in the treatment area and presence of skin infections
  • Oncology

Shaving the processing zone

Shave the hair in the area where laser hair removal will be performed a few days before the procedure to ensure optimal results.

Avoid Lotions and Similar Products

Before the procedure, avoid using creams, lotions, or deodorants in the area to be treated, as some substances may affect the effectiveness of laser hair removal.

Avoiding Sun Exposure

Refrain from sunbathing or using tanning beds before the procedure, as tanning can increase the risk of irritation and pigmentation changes.

Medical Condition Check

Before starting the procedure, consult with a doctor or licensed professional to determine suitability and discuss potential risks and side effects.

Consultation with a Specialist

Before the procedure begins, the client receives a consultation from a licensed specialist, where expected results, safety concerns, and individual characteristics are discussed.

Skin Preparation

Prior to the procedure, the skin in the treatment area is cleansed and dehydrated to enhance the penetration of laser radiation.

Equipment Adjustment

The specialist sets the parameters of the laser, such as intensity and wavelength, according to the patient's skin type and hair characteristics.

Procedure Execution

Epilation is performed by directing laser radiation at the hair follicles, where the laser energy penetrates and destroys the hair down to the root.

Cooling and Soothing

During the procedure, a cooling system is used to reduce discomfort and prevent skin irritation.

Post-Procedure Care

The specialist provides recommendations for care, including avoiding sunlight and using creams to reduce potential irritations.

First Session

The initial stage where the parameters of laser hair removal are determined and a test session is conducted. This may include consultation, determining skin type, and testing the laser's impact on a small area.

Main Session Series

Over 4-8 sessions with regular intervals (usually 4-6 weeks), the main series of procedures is conducted. The frequency of sessions may vary depending on the treatment area and the client's individual characteristics.

Maintenance Sessions

After completing the main series of sessions, it is recommended to periodically undergo maintenance sessions to sustain the results. Intervals between such sessions may range from 2 to 6 months.

Evaluation of Results

Upon completion of the main course of laser hair removal, an evaluation of the results is conducted, and the specialist advises the client on further care and the possible need for additional procedures.

Use a non-greasy soothing cream or gel with aloe vera to reduce potential irritation and prevent redness.

If you experience mild itching, you can use antihistamine preparations or remedies recommended by a specialist.

On the first day after the procedure, it's better to avoid hot baths and jacuzzis, as heat can irritate the skin.

After laser hair removal, avoid prolonged sun exposure for several days. If you go outside, use high sun protection products.

During the week after laser hair removal, avoid friction or rubbing in the treated area.

For several days after the procedure, limit intense physical activity to avoid skin irritation and maintain results.
